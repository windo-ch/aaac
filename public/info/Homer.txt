Homer
2024
glazed ceramic
21 × 20 cm (8.3 × 7.9 in)
3 kg (6.6 lb)


In Homer, Dimitria Barrows transforms a viral internet GIF from The Simpsons into a sculptural ceramic vessel. The work references the widely circulated image of Homer Simpson disappearing into a hedge-an enduring meme symbolizing social withdrawal, awkwardness, and digital-era self-erasure.
The cylindrical form is fully encrusted with individually handmade ceramic leaves, glazed in layered greens and carved with detailed veining. From within this dense foliage, the cartoon figure emerges mid-disappearance, creating a visual tension between flat, digital animation and tactile, hand-built clay. The labor-intensive surface contrasts with the immediacy of meme culture, translating a looping digital image into a singular, materially grounded artwork.
Open and hollow, the vessel reinforces themes of concealment and absence. By merging contemporary internet culture with traditional ceramic techniques, Barrows bridges digital and physical realms, situating meme imagery within the lineage of contemporary ceramic sculpture.
